# Ad Schedule Research - Preliminary Assessment
## NOT FACT — Based on third-party data, not Ridgecrest-specific performance
### Saved: April 11, 2026 8:29 AM PDT

---

## Purpose
Henry observed that throughout his career, leads came in on weekends via organic website traffic. This research evaluates whether the Fri/Sat/Sun/Mon Google Ads schedule makes sense, or whether we're leaving money on the table Tue-Thu.

---

## Third-Party Data Sources

### 1. Zahavian Legal Marketing - Google Ads Conversion Data
- Source: https://www.zahavianlegalmarketing.com/google-adwords-best-time-schedule/
- Tuesday: 22.5% of conversions (highest day)
- Wednesday: 18.3%
- Monday: 16.7%
- Friday: 14.2%
- Thursday: 10.8%
- Saturday: 9.2%
- Sunday: 8.3%
- Weekdays produced 82.5% of all lead conversions
- Weekday average: 16.5% per day vs Weekend average: 8.75% per day

### 2. Conversion Surgery - Weekend Contractor Leads (March 2026)
- Source: https://conversionsurgery.io/blog/weekend-inquiry-playbook-saturday-leads
- Weekend contractor leads have HIGHER INTENT than weekday leads
- Homeowners have time to research, compare, and decide on Saturdays

### 3. RapportAgent - After-Hours Lead Study (10,847 inquiries, Feb 2026)
- Source: https://www.rapportagent.com/blog/after-hours-home-service-lead-study/
- 62% of all home service inquiries come OUTSIDE business hours (9-5)
- Saturday is just as busy as a weekday
- Sunday only slightly behind
- After-hours leads are 38% more valuable ($847 avg vs $612 business hours)
- After-hours leads have higher urgency, larger job value, faster decision-making

### 4. ClicksGeek - Google Ads for Home Improvement (April 9, 2026)
- Source: https://clicksgeek.com/google-ads-for-home-improvement-companies/
- 6 PM to 10 PM: clicks convert at premium rates (homeowners researching after work)
- Recommend +15-25% bid adjustment during peak evening hours
- 2 AM to 6 AM: generates clicks but zero quality leads — reduce bids 50% or pause

### 5. Mr Search - Weekend Advertising Analysis
- Source: https://mrsearch.com.au/should-you-run-google-ads-advertising-on-weekends/
- Significant number of people Google on weekends to research, then CALL Monday/Tuesday
- Weekend visibility feeds Monday lead volume
- Weekends are almost always quieter (about half the search activity of weekdays)
- But quieter does not mean not worthwhile

### 6. Reddit r/googleads - Service Business Weekend Split Discussion (Sept 2025)
- Source: https://www.reddit.com/r/googleads/comments/1nf04lo/should_i_split_my_google_ads_campaign_into/
- Advertiser confirmed weekends attract MORE leads with LESS competition
- Weekdays (Mon-Thu) spend same budget but generate fewer leads
- Community consensus: don't split campaigns, use bid adjustments instead
- Google favors consistency over on/off switching

---

## Key Takeaways (Preliminary)

1. Henry's instinct about weekends is validated — weekend leads have higher intent and less competition
2. BUT weekday volume is significantly higher (82.5% of conversions in one study)
3. Running ONLY Fri-Mon means Ridgecrest is invisible Tue-Thu when the majority of total searches happen
4. Evening hours (6-10 PM) are peak conversion windows across all studies
5. Weekend research often converts to Monday/Tuesday phone calls — so weekend ads feed early-week leads
6. The right approach is likely 7-day coverage with bid adjustments, not a 4-day blackout

---

## Decision Made
- Expanding from Fri/Sat/Sun/Mon to 7 days
- Tue/Wed/Thu at -25% bid adjustment (reduced but not zero)
- Evening 6-10 PM at +20% bid adjustment (all days)
- Fri/Sat/Sun/Mon remain at full bid
- Will reassess after 2-3 weeks of data to see actual Ridgecrest conversion patterns by day/hour
