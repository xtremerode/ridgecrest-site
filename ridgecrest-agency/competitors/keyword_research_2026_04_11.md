## Keywords to AVOID (Low Intent / Wrong Audience)
- Anything with: DIY, ideas, inspiration, photos, pictures, magazine, how to, tutorial
- Anything with: jobs, hiring, careers, salary, employment
- Anything with: wholesale, supplies, materials, depot, lumber
- Anything with: free, cheap, affordable, budget, low cost
- Anything with: small bathroom remodel costs, cost calculator (price shoppers, not luxury)
- Anything with: TV show, home improvement cast, Tim Allen (entertainment searches)

## Strategy Notes

### From BG Collective (bgcollective.com) - Google Ads for Remodelers 2026:
- "72% of all booked remodeling consultations come from high-intent search terms that include a city or project type"
- High-intent keywords cost more per click but represent homeowners already committed to hiring
- "A remodeler spending $8,000 per month does not need hundreds of terms. They need a tight cluster."
- Phrases like "affordable remodeling" or "free estimates" invite the wrong conversations
- One $6M design-build client stopped using price language, reduced lead volume 22%, but INCREASED revenue

### From ClicksGeek (clicksgeek.com) - April 9, 2026:
- Formula: [service] + [buying signal] + [location]
- "30 laser-focused keywords outperform 300 loosely relevant ones"
- Start with phrase match, expand to exact match for maximum control
- Build initial list with 20-30 high-intent terms per service type

### From CurbSide Search (curbsidesearch.com) - April 6, 2026:
- Luxury-specific keywords pre-qualify leads by budget before they ever contact you
- "luxury kitchen remodel [city]" and "high-end whole-home renovation contractor" carry built-in budget signals
- "A homeowner who searches 'luxury design-build contractor [city]' has already signaled their intent and their budget tier"
