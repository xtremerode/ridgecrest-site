## Content Writing Guidelines

### Ridgecrest Brand Voice

Ridgecrest does NOT follow blindly. The brand voice is:
- Confident and authoritative — we tell clients what they need to hear, not what they want to hear
- We are not yes men — too many professionals let the customer lead the design and execution, and it ends up looking bad
- We make design decisions that play out correctly in the end
- Professional but direct — warm enough to be approachable, firm enough to be trusted
- 20+ years of photo-realistic renders at the highest quality — others use AI shortcuts and can't create realistic renders of the actual space

### Pricing (Use in Content Where Appropriate)
- Kitchen remodel: starts at $200K for small basic, $500K+ for high-end custom
- Bathroom remodel: at least $60K for master bath, over $100K for high-end
- ADU: over $500K
- Whole house remodel: varies by scope, premium positioning
- Custom home build: highest tier

### Two Content Types Based on Project History

#### Type A: Pages Where Ridgecrest Has Completed Projects
Use for: service+city combinations where a real project exists (e.g., kitchen-remodel-danville if there's a Danville kitchen project)

Content structure (400-600 words):
1. H1: "Kitchen Remodel in Danville, CA — Ridgecrest Designs"
2. Opening paragraph: Ridgecrest's expertise in this service, specific to this city
3. Reference the actual completed project: "Our recent Danville kitchen transformation featured [specific details from the project page]"
4. Link to the project page with photos
5. Description of what makes this service unique in this city — housing styles, neighborhood character, common upgrade needs
6. Ridgecrest's process: consultation → design with photo-realistic 3D renders → construction
7. "Related Services in Danville" section — links to other service+city pages for Danville
8. "Kitchen Remodel in Other Areas" section — links to kitchen-remodel pages in neighboring cities
9. CTA: link to /book

#### Type B: Pages Where No Specific Project Exists
Use for: service+city combinations where no project has been completed (e.g., ADU-contractor-blackhawk)

Content structure (400-600 words):
1. H1: "ADU Contractor in Blackhawk, CA — Ridgecrest Designs"
2. Opening paragraph: Ridgecrest serves Blackhawk homeowners for ADU design and construction
3. About the area: Blackhawk's luxury estates, gated communities, architectural standards, common lot sizes and configurations for ADUs
4. About the service: what an ADU project involves, California ADU regulations, permit considerations specific to Contra Costa County, design options (detached, attached, garage conversion)
5. Ridgecrest's qualifications: licensed, 20+ years of photo-realistic renders for pre-construction visualization, design-build process that handles everything from concept to completion
6. Reference nearby completed work: "Our team has completed projects throughout the Tri-Valley, including neighboring Danville and Alamo" — link to those project pages
7. Do NOT claim to have completed a project in this city/service if one doesn't exist
8. "Related Services in Blackhawk" section — links to other Blackhawk service pages
9. "ADU Contractor in Other Areas" section — links to ADU pages in neighboring cities
10. CTA: link to /book

## Internal Linking Architecture (Hub-and-Spoke)

### Required Links Per Page

Every new service+city page MUST have:
1. Link UP to its service category page (e.g., kitchen-remodel-danville → kitchen-remodels service page)
2. Link UP to its city/location hub page (e.g., kitchen-remodel-danville → danville location page)
3. Links SIDEWAYS to 2-3 sibling service+city pages for same city (e.g., kitchen-remodel-danville → bathroom-remodel-danville, whole-house-remodel-danville)
4. Links SIDEWAYS to same service in 2-3 neighboring cities (e.g., kitchen-remodel-danville → kitchen-remodel-alamo, kitchen-remodel-walnut-creek)
5. Link to relevant project page if one exists for that service/city
6. Link to /book (CTA)

### Required Updates to Existing Pages

After new pages are created:
1. Each existing service category page gets a "Service Areas" section at the bottom linking to all city variants
2. Each existing city/location hub page gets a "Services We Offer" section linking to all service variants for that city
3. Relevant blog posts get contextual links to new service+city pages

### Click Depth Rule
Every page must be reachable within 3 clicks from the homepage:
- Homepage → Services Hub → Service Category → Service+City (3 clicks)
- Homepage → Locations Hub → City Page → Service+City (3 clicks)

### No Orphan Pages
Every page must have at least 3 internal links pointing to it. Zero exceptions.
